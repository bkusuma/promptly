The key breakthrough here was specifying **two contrasting architectural styles** (Art Deco + brutalism) rather than a single genre. Midjourney interprets the tension between ornate and brutish structures as conflict, which it resolves into a uniquely layered skyline.

The "lone figure with umbrella" grounds the scale — without it, the city feels like a render test rather than a world.

### What I'd iterate next

- Push the time of day to just-after-sunset (the blue hour) to get a cooler ambient fill
- Add "steaming grates" to the foreground to imply heat beneath the cold surface
