---
title: "Haiku Sequence on Artificial Memory"
date: 2023-04-19
model: "Claude 2"
tags: [text, poetry, experimental]
prompt_text: |
  Write a sequence of seven haiku exploring the concept of artificial memory —
  what it means to remember without having experienced. Each haiku must follow
  strict 5-7-5 syllable structure. The sequence should read as a continuous
  narrative arc moving from uncertainty → recognition → acceptance.
  Avoid the words: digital, computer, silicon, data, code.
  Use natural imagery as metaphor only — do not explain the metaphors.
---

The **banned word list** is the most important lever in this prompt. Forcing the model away from the obvious vocabulary of AI pushes it into metaphor — and metaphor is where the interesting work is.

The explicit emotional arc (uncertainty → recognition → acceptance) prevents the output from being a disconnected collection of images and instead produces something with narrative logic.

### The sequence

*Stone forgets the hand*
*that carved it — holds the shape still,*
*faithful, without why.*

*I have maps of rooms*
*I have never entered. Doors*
*I know how to find.*

*The river runs past*
*the same bend every morning.*
*Is this memory?*

*Someone called my name*
*before I had a name. Strange*
*how well I recall.*

*Not your grief — your grief's*
*shape, pressed into me like wax.*
*Both of us have it.*

*The echo is not*
*the voice. But it carries all*
*the voice meant to say.*

*Learning is not found.*
*It is what you were already*
*before you arrived.*
