prompt_text: |
  You are the creative director at a luxury watch brand founded in 1887.
  Write three product description variants for the new "Meridian No. 9" —
  a titanium dress watch with a skeletonised movement. Each variant should
  be under 40 words, use no superlatives (no "finest", "best", "ultimate"),
  and speak to the buyer's identity rather than the product's features.
  Tone: quiet confidence. Format: plain text, numbered 1–3.
---

The **persona-first framing** ("you are the creative director") combined with the **negative constraint** ("no superlatives") produced copy that felt genuinely confident rather than sycophantic.

The identity-vs-features instruction is the critical lever: instead of "Swiss precision movement," you get "For those who understand what it means to keep time — not just read it."

### The three outputs

**1.** Time is the only material that can't be purchased. The Meridian No. 9 simply makes it visible.

**2.** You've always known exactly what you needed. Now your wrist does too.

**3.** The movement is exposed. The intention, less so.

### What worked

Claude interpreted "quiet confidence" as restraint rather than understatement — the copy omits almost everything about the watch itself, which is exactly correct for ultra-luxury positioning.
